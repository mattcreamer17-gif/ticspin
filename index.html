The 60/30/10 frame, applied to TicSpin

Most of what a one-person retailer does is not an AI problem. It is straightforward software and process work. Sixty percent of the gains come from tighter listing workflows, content operations, and delivery scheduling that a traditional build can handle. Thirty percent comes from rules: verification, dispute handling, seller onboarding. Only the last ten percent is the kind of work where agents earn their keep: automated listing drafts from phone photos, customer support triage, catalog enrichment at scale. Teams that skip this distinction spend their first hire on the wrong tool.

A recent example

We worked with a 46-property short-term rental operator in a similar shape: small team, heavy ops, a backlog of ideas that did not fit the team they had. We scoped the work in two tiers so the first layer paid for itself before the second tiered on top. The engagement was a scoped sequence, audit first, then playbook delivery. We did not sell a custom agent build. We sold the sequence.
